About

I am a predoctoral researcher at CIC biomaGUNE working on the development of disease-modifying strategies for Alzheimer’s disease using molecular imaging. My research combines PET, MRI and MRS with behavioural and biochemical analyses to study amyloid pathology, neuroinflammation and therapeutic responses in preclinical models.

More broadly, I am interested in translational neuroimaging, neurodegenerative diseases and scientific communication, particularly through clear data visualization.

Research Interests

  • Molecular imaging of Alzheimer's disease
  • Preclinical PET imaging
  • Multimodal neuroimaging (PET, MRI, MRS)
  • Neuroinflammation and amyloid pathology
  • Translational neuroimaging

Current Research

My PhD project evaluates transthyretin stabilizers as potential disease-modifying drugs for Alzheimer's disease using longitudinal multimodal imaging in transgenic mouse models.
