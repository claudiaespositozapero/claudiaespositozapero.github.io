About

My work focuses on combining in vivo imaging with molecular and biochemical techniques to better understand neurodegeneration and evaluate novel therapeutic approaches.

I am currently pursuing a PhD in Synthetic and Industrial Chemistry at CIC biomaGUNE, funded by an FPI predoctoral fellowship. My research explores transthyretin stabilizers as potential disease-modifying therapies for Alzheimer's disease using a multimodal imaging approach. Before this, I worked in neuropharmacology at the University of Valencia studying the mechanisms of ethanol action in the mesocorticolimbic dopaminergic system.

Research focus

A combination of molecular imaging, neurobiology, and translational questions.

Molecular imaging in Alzheimer's disease

Longitudinal PET imaging to assess amyloid burden and neuroinflammation in transgenic mouse models.

Multimodal neuroimaging

MRI and MRS to study structural and metabolic brain changes during disease progression and treatment.

Ex vivo validation and biomarkers

Immunohistochemistry, ELISA, flow cytometry and cytokine multiplex assays to validate in vivo findings.
