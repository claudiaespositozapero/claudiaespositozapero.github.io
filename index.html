Claudia Espósito

PhD candidate in Molecular Imaging at CIC biomaGUNE (San Sebastián, Spain). My research focuses on the evaluation of transthyretin stabilizers as disease-modifying strategies for Alzheimer’s disease using multimodal molecular imaging.

I combine PET, MRI and MRS imaging with behavioural studies and ex vivo analyses to investigate amyloid pathology, neuroinflammation and therapeutic responses in preclinical models.

More broadly, I am interested in translational neuroimaging, neurodegeneration research and clear scientific communication, particularly through effective data visualization.

Research Interests

  • Molecular imaging of neurodegenerative diseases
  • Alzheimer’s disease and amyloid pathology
  • Preclinical PET imaging
  • Multimodal neuroimaging (PET, MRI, MRS)
  • Translational neuroimaging

Current Position

Predoctoral Researcher
Radiochemistry and Nuclear Imaging Lab
CIC biomaGUNE, San Sebastián (Spain)
Supervisors: Jordi Llop & Abraham Martín

My PhD project evaluates transthyretin stabilizers as potential disease-modifying drugs for Alzheimer’s disease using longitudinal imaging studies in transgenic mouse models.

Background

Before starting my PhD, I worked in the Neuropharmacology Group at the University of Valencia, studying the mechanisms of ethanol action in the mesocorticolimbic dopaminergic system using behavioural and neurochemical approaches.

I hold a Pharmacy degree from the University of Valencia and I am currently pursuing a PhD in Synthetic and Industrial Chemistry funded by an FPI fellowship.
