Current mission

Investigating disease-modifying strategies for Alzheimer’s disease using multimodal molecular imaging. My work combines PET, MRI and MRS with behavioural and biochemical analyses in preclinical models.

Research journey

Foundation in pharmacology, biomedical sciences and experimental research, providing the scientific background that later shaped my transition into neuroscience and molecular imaging.

Pharmacology Biomedical sciences Lab training

Research focused on the mechanisms of ethanol action using behavioural and neurochemical approaches in rodent models, including experimental surgery and in vivo techniques.

Behavioural assays Stereotaxic surgery Microdialysis Jugular cannulation

Current PhD research focused on transthyretin stabilizers as disease-modifying strategies for Alzheimer’s disease, combining longitudinal imaging, behavioural studies and ex vivo validation.
