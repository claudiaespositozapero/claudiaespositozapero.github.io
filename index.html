Hi, I'm Claudia. I study Alzheimer's disease using molecular imaging (and quite a lot of mice).

I am a predoctoral researcher at CIC biomaGUNE, working on the effects of transthyretin stabilizers in a mouse model of Alzheimer's disease. My research combines molecular imaging, behavioral studies, and biochemical analyses to better understand disease mechanisms and treatment responses.

About

My work focuses on combining in vivo imaging with molecular and biochemical techniques to better understand neurodegenerative diseases and evaluate new therapeutic strategies. I am particularly interested in integrating multiple data types to build a clearer picture of disease progression.

Research

Molecular imaging in Alzheimer's disease

Longitudinal PET imaging to evaluate amyloid plaques and neuroinflammation in mouse models of Alzheimer's disease.

Multimodal neuroimaging

Using MRI and MRS to study structural and metabolic changes in the brain during disease progression.

Biomarkers and ex vivo validation

Immunohistochemistry, ELISA, transthyretin measurements and cytokine multiplex assays to validate in vivo findings.
